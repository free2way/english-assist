# AceEnglish Product Introduction Deck Plan

Audience: non-technical stakeholders, teachers, parents, school administrators, and product partners who need to understand what the app does and why AI agent coordination matters.

Objective: introduce AceEnglish as a practical AI-powered English learning assistant, show real interface screenshots, explain the normal learning features, and make the AI/AI-agent implementation understandable without turning the deck into a technical audit.

Narrative arc:
1. English speaking practice is hard to sustain because learners need guidance, feedback, and follow-up.
2. AceEnglish turns textbook units into a guided daily learning loop.
3. The app combines normal learning tools with AI speaking practice, pronunciation assessment, and teacher visibility.
4. The new multi-agent layer makes the app feel less like a chatbot and more like a coordinated learning team.
5. Teachers and learners both get actionable next steps.

Slide list:
1. Cover: AceEnglish as an AI English learning assistant.
2. What the app helps people do.
3. Student learning center screenshot and daily path.
4. Textbook synchronized practice screenshot and feature set.
5. AI speaking tutor screenshot.
6. How the AI agents cooperate.
7. Teacher insight screenshot.
8. Why this is practical AI, not just a chat window.
9. Closing value proposition.

Visual system:
- Modern product launch style.
- High contrast ink text, clean white canvas, electric blue, fresh green, coral and lemon accents.
- Screenshot-forward layouts with large framed UI images, floating labels, and simple geometric rhythm.
- Avoid the previous editorial paper style; use crisp product storytelling.
